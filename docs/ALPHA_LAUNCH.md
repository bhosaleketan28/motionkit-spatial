# Hoppy Alpha Launch

## Product Overview

Hoppy turns local images into cinematic motion in the browser. Testers can choose one of six motion systems, begin from one of 24 curated presets, adjust the result in a stage-first workspace, and export a WebM loop or PNG still without uploading media to a server.

## Who Should Test

- product designers presenting interface or product work;
- brand and campaign designers building motion-ready visual sequences;
- editorial and social designers looking for fast spatial presentation formats;
- creative technologists evaluating browser-based motion workflows.

## Recommended Setup

Use a current Chrome or Chromium-based browser on desktop or laptop for the most reliable WebM path. Safari and Firefox remain manual-verification targets. Local images remain in browser memory only and must be added again after reload.

## Tester Goal

Create one useful motion output in under five minutes.

## Suggested Flow

1. Start with one of the three showcase scenarios.
2. Browse the motion library and choose a system that fits the content.
3. Try one of its four curated presets.
4. Modify at least two motion, layout, or appearance controls.
5. Play and scrub through a complete loop.
6. Export a WebM. Optionally compare it with a PNG still.

## Showcase Scenarios

- **Product Showcase:** Focus Deck, Product Hero, and the Forma One demo set.
- **Editorial Story:** Film Strip, Editorial Flow, and the Northline Editorial demo set.
- **Brand Campaign:** Grid Wall, Editorial Wall, and the Atlas System demo set.

## Known Limitations

- WebM is the only video format; PNG exports one still frame and is not a video replacement.
- Browser WebM codec and canvas-recording support varies and is checked at export time.
- MP4 is not available.
- Uploaded media does not persist across reloads.
- There is no project saving, cloud storage, timeline, layers, keyframes, collaboration, or account system.
- Transparent WebM playback can vary between browsers.
- Safari and Firefox upload, focus, color, and export behavior still need direct manual verification.

## Feedback Questions

- What were you trying to create with Hoppy?
- Which motion system did you choose?
- What felt confusing?
- Would you use Hoppy for real work?
- What output did you create?

## Investor Or Stakeholder Walkthrough

1. Open first run and state the promise in one sentence.
2. Launch Product Showcase to demonstrate a complete result immediately.
3. Open the motion library to show six distinct systems and 24 curated presets.
4. Change a preset and two inspector controls while the stage updates live.
5. Scrub, fit the stage, and enter focus mode.
6. Open export review, explain the local browser-only model, and export WebM.
7. Switch to Orbit Carousel and show a structurally different second output.
8. Show the Alpha guide to make constraints and the feedback path explicit.

## Validation Baseline

The controlled-alpha pass covers 1440×900, 1280×720, 960×800, 768×1024, and 390×844 in the Chromium-based in-app browser. Safari and Firefox remain named manual follow-ups rather than implied coverage.
